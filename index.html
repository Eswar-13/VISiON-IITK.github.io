Who Are We?

Team VISiON (previously Team IGVC-IITK), a robotics team from IIT Kanpur is a group of highly motivated students with a passion for robotics consisting of Undergraduate Students and Faculty Advisor, working in the field of autonomous ground vehicles to participate in the Intelligent Ground Vehicle Competition (IGVC), which is being held at Oakland University, Michigan, USA pointer-eventsry year in June for the past 26 years. Approximately 40 teams typically from India, Europe, and the USA participate in this competition. We are also aiming to participate in NASA Space Robotics Challenge which is a virtual competition to advance robotic software and autonomous capabilities for space exploration missions on the surface of extraterrestrial objects, such as distant planets or moons.

What we do here ...

Computer Vision

Computer vision is an interdisciplinary scientific field that deals with how computers can be made to gain a high-level understanding from digital images or videos. This domain forms a very integral component of our setup. Our endeavor to make a self navigable robot involves first taking input from the local surroundings and then making sense of it. Currently, we have implemented Fast Segmentation Neural Network (Fast SCNN).

Its selling point over conventional neural network models is that it parses inputs quickly to facilitate quick responsive interactivity with the environment. Our Fast-SCNN uses learning to downsample module, a coarse global feature extractor, a feature fusion module, and a standard classifier. This is a very recent and sophisticated model and the current industry standard.

Simultaneous Localization and Mapping (SLAM)

SLAM is one of the hardest parts of autonomous navigation as it involves mapping the environment while also simultaneously keeping track of bots position in it. We use data from a 2D-lidar sensor for detecting obstacles and combine it with the current motion of the robot to predict the map and location of the bot in it. We use Bayesian probabilistic models for it and for approximating the equations we apply Kalman filters.

We are actively exploring and testing new SLAM algorithms like using Particle Filters(Monte Carlo methods) in place of Kalman filters or completely moving to visual based SLAM algorithms or integrating these two.

Path Planning and Control

The algorithm that we use in path planning is Bi-RRT (Bidirectional Rapidly Exploring Random Tree), which uses the map , bot and target position generated by SLAM algorithm.RRT algorithm was chosen over others because it is fast and non-holonomic constraints could easily be applied to it. The tree is constructed incrementally from samples drawn randomly from the search space and is inherently biased to grow towards large unsearched areas of the problem. The algorithm gives us a piecewise straight path between source and target. We can have an infinite angular acceleration of bot at corners and hence spline interpolation technique is applied on the path. This technique is used to make the path smooth in such a way that it becomes second order continuous and hence prevents the existence of infinite acceleration in the entire path. In the future, we aim to use various curve fitting like polynomial, Gaussian and Fourier fitting individually or simultaneously on nodes generated by RRT and use methods like LAR and Bi-Square to improve robustness of the generated path.

For high-level path following controller, a non-linear unicycle controller was chosen over the usual PID as it performed decently and was easier to tune.

Future aspects of team

Cyber Security

We are keen to work in the field of cybersecurity. We participated in the cybersecurity challenge of IGVC 2019 and secured 4th rank worldwide. We now have a subgroup in our team which devotes itself towards solving challenges in cybersecurity. We are looking forward to exploring and contribute toward cybersecurity.

Self-driving Car

Autonomous vehicle i.e. the self driving car is the future of the automobile industry. We are aiming to participate in prominent competitions that involve developing hardware and algorithms for the self-driving car.

Simulations of complex robots

We can't work on space rovers, humanoid bots, or autonomous rockets due to budget and time contraints. Well think again, with proper hardware we can simulate those to well enough accuracy. Our team has fervent interest in software simulation of the complex bots used in various disciplines like space rover, reinforced humanoid, etc.
